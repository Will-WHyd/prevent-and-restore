Understanding radicalization

Radicalisation is the word commonly used to describe the mental process the person is going through as they get drawn down a dangerous path. It can be hard to spot or understand, but by understanding the signs, you can help those at risk.

The PREVENT strategy aims to help provide supports and stop those at risk from becoming radicalized.


What is Prevent?

The UK's PREVENT strategy is part of the government's broader counter-terrorism strategy known as CONTEST. Launched in 2003, PREVENT aims to stop individuals from becoming terrorists or supporting terrorism.

PREVENT's key aspects:

Objectives

PREVENT seeks to safeguard vulnerable individuals from being radicalized and drawn into terrorism. It focuses on early intervention and community engagement.

Approach

The strategy emphasizes collaboration between various sectors, including education, health, local authorities, and law enforcement. It encourages professionals to identify and support at-risk individuals.

Channel Program

A key component of PREVENT, the strategy offerd tailored support to individuals identified as being at risk of radicalization. It involves multi-agency panels that assess risks and provide appropriate interventions and community support.

Legislation

The Counter-Terrorism and Security Act 2015 placed a statutory duty on various institutions, including schools and healthcare providers, to prevent radicalization.

Recognise the Signs of Radicalization

In a complex world, there are many factors that can make people vunerable to radicalization. They can apply to anyone, regardless of age, social class, religious, ethnic, or educational background.
